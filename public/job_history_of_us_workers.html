Job history of more than 1.2 million US workers

By Abdullah

I would like to share an interesting study from ADP this week! They analyzed the job history of more than 1.2 million US workers in the past 3 years and found that 29% of people quit their jobs within a month of their first promotion. Why's this? Economists have noted these newly promoted folks are overwhelmed + unsupported in their role and/or have newfound confidence to seek opportunities externally.

What does this mean for HR practitioners and managers? From experience, I've seen IC's transition into a leadership role without much support and often make avoidable mistakes. Simply put, HR + managers need to invest their time in building role transitional programs that help bridge the gap for the newly promoted.

Any other suggestions from my HR peers?
